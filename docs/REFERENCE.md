# Laser Eyes: Core Vision & Values

This document serves as the north star for the development of **Laser Eyes**. Every feature, update, and design choice should be weighed against these four pillars.

---

## 1. 🌿 HEALTHY (The "Refresh" Principle)
The primary physical goal of Laser Eyes is to provide a gaming experience that leaves the user feeling better than when they started.

- **Physical Differentiation**: Move away from sedentary "hunch and click" mechanics.
- **Micro-Ergonomics**: Specifically designed to encourage the relaxation of hands and wrists.
- **Active Movement**: Gameplay encourages gentle, purposeful neck rotation and (eventually) facial expression exercises.
- **Post-Play State**: A player should feel physically refreshed and loose after a session, as if they've completed a light physical therapy or relaxation routine.

## 2. 🎮 FUN (The "Flow" Principle)
While health is the goal, "Game" is the medium. It must be engaging enough to hide the effort.

- **Gamified Engagement**: Scoring, visual rewards, and clear objectives.
- **Challenging**: Mechanics should require focus and skill without becoming frustrating.
- **Adaptive Difficulty**: The game should sense the player's skill level and adjust target movement, count, and "lock-on" requirements to maintain the "flow state."

## 3. 🧠 UX IS KEY (The "Invisible Guide" Principle)
Interface complexity and textual clutter are the enemies of immersion.

- **Intuitive Discovery**: Players should learn how to play by playing. The UI should guide the eyes naturally.
- **Minimum Text**: Text is a failure of visual design. Keep instructions to the absolute minimum necessary.
- **Proactive Assistance**: The system must be "smart" enough to detect:
    - Calibration drift.
    - Loss of face/nose tracking.
    - Player confusion (e.g., staring at the wrong place).
- **Graceful Guidance**: Use colors, pulses, and arrows first. Only resort to text when visual cues fail.

## 4. ✨ POLISH (The "Premium" Principle)
The experience must feel like a high-end, futuristic tool, not a tech demo.

- **Slick & Responsive**: Zero-latency feel (or the illusion of it). High frame rates and smooth interpolations (like laser snapping).
- **Clean Aesthetic**: A minimalist, high-contrast look that feels "Apple-meets-Cyberpunk."
- **Micro-Animations**: Every interaction (a target appearing, a laser locking, a score popping) should have a satisfying visual and (if possible) haptic "crunch."
